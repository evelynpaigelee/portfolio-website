Care & Share

Role

UI/UX Designer

Tools

Figma

Timeline

June - July 2024 (7 weeks)

Overview

Caregiving can be an overwhelming and isolating experience. There are many areas of psychological, emotional, relational, financial transition both for the loved one being cared for and the caregiver. As a physical therapist in the acute care setting, I often see the reality of many individuals and family members have ot become caregivers for their loved one.

Problem

Many existing caregiving apps are targeted towards hiring caregivers or providing organizational features. However, there is a lack of apps that provide users connection ot the caregiver community and access ot resources and education.

Solution

Care & Share is a caregiving native app developed for both iOS and Android. It allows users to receive caregiver resources, browse and contribute to discussion boards, message other users, and access educational materials.

Research

I conducted competitor analysis on two prominent caregiving apps, CircleOf and ianaCare. The goal was ot gain insights into their key functionalities, strengths, weaknesses, and the challenges they face, which could inform potential solutions for the caregiving app market.

Overall, both apps emphasized task management, care plan organization, and communication among users' care teams. However, they lacked features that facilitate connection and sharing of caregiving experiences between users. Additionally, there was no centralized resource for educational videos and articles on caregiving and healthcare diagnoses.

User Personas

User Flow

Based on competitive research and the primary goal of developing an app that connects users to the caregiver community while providing access ot resources and education, | identified the following key functionalities:

  • Personalized Profiles: Allow user to select populations and topics to curate suggested content
  • Communication: Ability to write discussion posts and message other users
  • Educational Resources: Informative pages on caregiving topics and health diagnoses

Wireframe

Care & Share is a native app for iOS and Android, so ti was essential to adhere to both the iOS Human Interface Guidelines and the Android Material Design Guidelines. I considered various factors, including screen sizes, typography, shadows, motion, iconography, navigation, toolbars, and buttons. Using these guidelines, I developed mid-fidelity wireframes for both iOS and Android.

Style Guide

Following iOS Human Interface and the Android Material Design Guidelines, I utilized SF Pro for iOS and Roboto for Android typography.

This color palette features a blend of soft, earthy tones and cool, muted shades. The teal and gentle green evoke a sense of tranquility, while the subtle gray-blue and warm blush add warmth and sophistication. I designed this calming palette to bring a sense of peace to the caregiving app.

Usability Testing

I designed high-fidelity wireframes implementing the style guide. With the wireframes, I created a prototype for both OiS and android using Figma. I recruited 5subjects for user testing, gathered feedback, and addressed users pain points with solutions.

Pain Points
  1. Difficulty reading the login and register screen
  2. Android - Segmented picker on login screen si hard to read
  3. Visual crowding on homepage
  4. iOS - Challenging to see which tab is selected on bottom navigation bar, not enough contrast
  5. iOS & Android - Need more visual interest for education page to differentiate between diagnoses and types of transplants page

Solution

Changed introduction imagery and adjusted font sizes accordingly
Increased opacity of text holder to improve readability and added shapes for visual interest
Changed slider format to maintain consistency with picker on resources page
Increasing readability and reducing visual clutter on homepage by removing extra shapes and adjusting gradients
Increased visibility of selected tab in bottom navigation bar
Added icons for diagnoses and changed button shapes to increase visual variety and interest

Conclusion

I really enjoyed this project because it allowed me to merge my healthcare background with my Ul/UX design skills by creating a solution for caregivers seeking connection, community, and educational resources through my app, Care &Share. By utilizing resources and the Figma community, I successfully implemented the iOS Human Interface Guidelines and Android Material Design Guidelines ni developing this native app. I recognized the importance of maintaining visual simplicity in design to enhance functionality and usability.

I believe it would be beneficial to conduct user testing with patients and their families in the hospital to assess whether the app effectively meets their needs in real-time as they transition into caregiving roles. To continue to build on the current app, I would love ot add several features to the app, including multi-language support for both the app and its materials. Additionally, I envision collaborating with healthcare professionals to offer live Q&A sessions, webinars, and workshops that caregivers could access through the app.
